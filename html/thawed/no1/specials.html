Pumpkin Chiffon... My Favorite!

Another thing that is sort of special is that Thanksgiving is just around the corner. We are planning a "Special" Thanksgiving issue. (Well at least I am. Kevin and I are in a disagreement on the correct dates for thanksgiving. But I think we all know when the pilgrims REALLY landed!)

So here is a little recipe to try out courtesy of Robline my dear sister. It is called Pumpkin Chiffon Pie. Ummm... now doesn't that sound yummy? So here goes. 

1 1/4 c canned pumpkin
1 c brown sugar
1/2 c rich milk (who knows what this is?)
3 egg yolk, beaten well
1/2 tsp ground ginger
1 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
1 1/2 tsp ground allspice
1 1/4 tsp ground mace
1/2 tsp salt
1 tbsp gelatin soaked in 1/4 cold water
3 egg white 
3 tbsp sugar

Combine pumpkin, brown sugar, spices, salt, milk and beaten egg yolks
in top of a double boiler. Set over hot water and cook until thick. 
Soak gelatin in cold water for five minutes. Add to hot mixture and 
stir until thoroughly dissolved. Remove from burner. Cool.

Beat egg whites until stiff. Fold in sugar and beat until stiff 
peaks form. Fold into chilled pumpkin mixture. Pour into any 
desired baked pie shell. Chill. Garnish with whipped cream. 
Ginger snap crust goes very well.

Ginger Snap crust. (don't worry this is a quick one)

1 1/4 c finely crushed ginger snaps
3 tbsp sugar
1/4 c butter melted

Mix all ingredients thoroughly. Press into a 9 inch pie pan. 
Bake 5 minutes in a 350 degree oven.
