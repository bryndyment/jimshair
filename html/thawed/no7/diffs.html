One thing very unique thing about Iceland is its geothermal activity. I remember as a child hearing of some exotic island in the north where the sidewalks were naturally heated to melt the snow. Although this is only partly true for some of the main sidewalks downtown, all of the buildings here are naturally heated. That's right, no hot water tanks in the houses.

    All of Reykjavík is heated by the hot springs which lye underneath the city. It is a particularily active spot. The water comes out of the earth at about 100 C (212 F) and must be cooled before being pumped into the houses. As it is, the water comes out of the tap scalding hot and smells strongly of minerals such as sulphur.
