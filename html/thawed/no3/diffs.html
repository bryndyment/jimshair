    Quirky little speech thing:"Já"

    Pronounced "yow" it means yeah. And just like in casual English conversation, it is used to acknowledge that you understand what someone's saying. Like "yeah, uh-huh, yep" etc. There's one little difference though, it is very often pronounced on the inhale, making it sound much like the speaker is exasperated or excited. I first encountered it as I was explaining something to one of the guys here at Gottfolk. As I spoke, he would casually offer the "yow" as he drew breath. It sort of sounded like he was saying "how" in disbelief. Took me a while to get used to.


    Most Fashionable Item In Iceland

    The results from our fashion watch are in. We have been carefully studying the fashion scene and have noticed many sizzling euro styles. Most popular -and unexpected- accessory, however, is the pram. Yes that's right, baby carriages. We're not talking about those little fold-up jobies either. It's the old-school 1930's type that look like a hearse.

    We each went out and bought one this weekend just to fit in. You can't walk down the street without one and not have people looking at you oddly. The only problem is that we don't have anything to put it them. I was thinking along the lines of a anatomically correct doll. Kevin was thinking more along the lines of a roast. What do you think?
