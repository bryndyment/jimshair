Icelandic Food of the Week: Brennivín


The special food this week is actually a beverage. Brennivín, the Icelandic national drink. It is a schnaaps but not a flavoured schnaaps, unless you consider turpentine a flavour. Kept in the freezer it becomes syrupy and is served in shot glasses. It is often drank with shark meat or herring. It was the only alcohol available in Iceland at one time, and it was manufatured and distributed by the state. To suppress alcoholism they gave Brennivín a butt-ugly lable design and called it "black death". Consumption skyrocketed.
